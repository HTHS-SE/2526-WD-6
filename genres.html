Sci-fi Subgenres

  • Apocalyptic/Post Apocalyptic

  • Time Travel


Apocalyptic and Post Apocalyptic

This subgenre focuses on a world during or after an apocalypse, or on characters attempting to prevent one. Sources of apocalypses vary widely, from climate to medical to religious. Modern technology is often described as failing, forcing characters to explore the world in a completely new way.

Biopunk

Biopunk deals with the consequences of (near-future) biomedical advancements. Often these technologies are controlled by totalitarian governments or companies. The main characters often are attempting to rebel and gain access to biological advancements of their own.

Cyberpunk

Best known by its film Blade Runner, the cyberpunk genre is often set in a crumbling technologically advanced future. Many times, the difference between real and virtual is blurred. Characters typically are looking to upset the status quo of a dystopian society, either for the collective good or just for their own gain.

Dystopian

Dystopian novels imagine a world where people live in constant fear and are often dehumanized; the opposite of a utopia. The government often has complete control over the population. Although typically set in the far future, books often draw parallels to the world today.

Sci-fi Horror

As horror is so closely linked with sci-fi (fear of the unknown), the sci-fi horror genre looks to combine the two. Science and technology advancements turn against humanity and our noble hero looks to avoid a grisly death.

Time travel

Time travel is likely the most associated trope with science fiction. Characters explore the timeline(s), get caught in the past and future, and deal with paradoxes galore.
